    PROMETHEE Method Overview

    What is PROMETHEE?
    PROMETHEE (Preference Ranking Organization Method for Enrichment Evaluation) is a multi-criteria decision method that ranks alternatives based on pairwise comparisons. It calculates preference degrees between alternatives for each criterion, then aggregates them using weights.

    Key Concepts

    1. Preference Functions

    Define how preference increases with difference in criterion values:

    • Usual: Indifferent until one alternative strictly dominates. No parameters needed.
    • U-Shape: Indifference threshold (q). Becomes preference after threshold.
    • V-Shape: Preference threshold (p). Preference increases linearly with difference.
    • Level: Both indifference (q) and preference (p) thresholds. Partial preference between them.
    • Linear: Similar to V-shape but with smoother increase.
    • Gaussian: Smooth S-shaped curve with threshold parameter (s).

    2. Thresholds

    • Indifference Threshold (q): Maximum difference where alternatives are considered equally good.
    • Preference Threshold (p): Minimum difference for strict preference of one alternative over another.

    3. Flow Scores

    • Φ+ (Positive Flow): How much an alternative dominates others. Higher is better.
    • Φ- (Negative Flow): How much an alternative is dominated by others. Lower is better.
    • Φ (Net Flow): Final ranking score = Φ+ - Φ-. Higher ranking = better alternative.

    4. Weights

    Criteria importance (must sum to 100%). Higher weight = more important criterion.

    For Military Applications

    • Targeting: Rank potential targets by threat level, accessibility, strategic value.
    • Force Selection: Compare available assets by capability, readiness, sustainability.
    • Course of Action: Evaluate different operational plans across multiple objectives.
    • Resource Allocation: Prioritize deployment or investment across criteria.
